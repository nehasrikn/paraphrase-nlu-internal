Instructions:

Are AI systems sensitive to word choice or sentence structure?

Ideally, when AI systems are presented with two snippets of text that convey the same meaning yet utilize different words or have different sentence structure, they should behave in a similar way. In this HIT, we'll present a scenario consisting of 3 sentences, and your task will be to write paraphrases for one portion of it (i.e use different words or sentence structure to convey the same meaning).

Each scenario consists of two premises and an evidence sentence that either strengthens or weakens the second premise sentence. The two premises set the stage for the scenario. Strengthening evidence sentences make your belief in the second premise sentence stronger, and weakening evidence sentences make your belief in the second premise sentence weaker.

Your task is to provide three paraphrases for an evidence sentence such that the words or sentence structure substantially differ from the original sentence, but the meaning is retained.

Your paraphrases should:
  • Be full, grammatical, coherent sentences.
  • Preserve the meaning of the sentence.
    • Make sure that sentence meaning does not differ from the original sentence.
    • Refrain from introducing new information that violates the situation evoked by the scenario.
    • Preserve weakening or strengthening evidence. If the evidence sentence is labeled as “strengthening”, your paraphrase should still strengthen your belief in the second premise sentence within the context of the scenario. If the sentence is labeled as “weakening” (i.e, it weakens your belief in the second premise sentence), make sure that your paraphrase retains this property.
  • Be as different as possible from the other paraphrases you enter.
  • Differ as much as possible from the original sentence.
    • Reduce word overlap between the original sentence and your paraphrased version.
    • The HIT UI includes a calculator to measure the distance between your paraphrase and the original sentence via word overlap (# of words in common with the original sentence). The higher this value is, the better! You don't need to achieve a distance measure of 100% in your paraphrases (since you might use words like "be", or use common proper nouns), but aim to keep this metric above 40%!) We indicate this ideal range with green (40%+), yellow (20%-40%), and red (0%-20%) coloring.
